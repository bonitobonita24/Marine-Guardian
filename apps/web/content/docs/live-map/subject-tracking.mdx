# Subject Tracking

A **subject** is anything Marine Guardian tracks on the map — a patrol boat, a ranger, or a marine animal. Subjects are organized into **subject groups**, and each subject accumulates **observations** (recorded position/sighting records) over time.

Subjects and their positions are read through the `map` router's `subjects` sub-router for plotting, and through the `subject` and `observation` routers for detail.

## Who uses this section

Operators locating tracked assets and animals, and field coordinators reviewing where a subject has been. Requires **view** on the `subjects` feature.

## Subjects on the map

The Live Map plots each subject's current position from the `subjects` sub-router. Subjects can be filtered by group so you can isolate, for example, just patrol boats or just a monitored species.

## Subject groups

The `subject.groups` procedure returns the visible subject groups, each with a count of how many subjects it contains. Groups are the primary way to organize and filter the subject layer.

## Browsing and searching subjects

The `subject.list` procedure powers the subject browser with:

- **Search** by name.
- **Filter** by group (`groupId`) or by active status (`isActive`).
- **Infinite scroll** via cursor pagination.

## Subject detail & observations

Selecting a subject opens its detail via `subject.getById`, which includes:

- The subject's **group**.
- Its most recent **observations**, newest first.

The `observation.list` procedure can also list observations directly, optionally filtered to a single subject (`subjectId`). Each observation is a recorded sighting/position for its subject.

## Key actions

1. Open the Live Map and enable the subjects layer.
2. Filter by **subject group** or active status to focus the map.
3. Select a subject to open its detail and review its recent observations.
