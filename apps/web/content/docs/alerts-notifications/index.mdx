Marine Guardian's alerting has three parts working together:

- **Alert Rules** decide *what* is worth an alert.
- **Alert History** is the log of every time a rule fired, with acknowledgement.
- The **Notification Center** is where each user reads the messages sent to them.

## Who uses it

- **Operators and coordinators** watch alerts, acknowledge them, and clear their
  notifications.
- **Site admins / super admins** create and edit alert rules and acknowledge
  alert-history entries.

## Notification channels

Alerts are delivered over two active channels in v1:

- **In-app** — surfaced in the [Notification Center](/docs/alerts-notifications/notifications).
- **Email** — sent via SMTP.

Every rule must have at least one channel selected.

> **SMS is planned, not active.** SMS delivery (Twilio and MSG91) is scaffolded
> in the codebase but is **not enabled in v1**. Only in-app and email alerts are
> sent today.

## How it flows

1. An admin defines an [Alert Rule](/docs/alerts-notifications/alert-rules) with a
   condition and one or more channels.
2. When incoming activity matches the condition, an alert fires and is recorded
   in [Alert History](/docs/alerts-notifications/alert-history).
3. The configured channels deliver the alert — an in-app notification lands in
   each recipient's [Notification Center](/docs/alerts-notifications/notifications),
   and/or an email is sent.
4. A responder **acknowledges** the alert so the team knows it's being handled.
