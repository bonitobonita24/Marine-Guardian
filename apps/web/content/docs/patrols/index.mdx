# Patrols

The Patrols section covers everything about where and when your rangers operate on the water and on foot. It brings together three related surfaces:

- **Patrol Monitoring** — review every patrol that has happened, drill into a single patrol's details, GPS track, and edit history.
- **Patrol Area Planning (Boundaries)** — define and maintain the municipalities, official boundaries, and protected zones that patrols and reports are measured against.
- **Patrol Schedule** — plan upcoming ranger assignments across a calendar, kanban board, map, and Gantt timeline.

## Where patrol data comes from

Most patrols are **ingested automatically from EarthRanger**. Marine Guardian pulls patrol records and their GPS tracks during sync, so the Patrols list fills in without manual entry. You can still make targeted local edits (see [Patrol Detail](/docs/patrols/patrol-detail)) — those edits are protected from being overwritten by future syncs.

Patrols are classified by two properties:

- **Type** — `foot` or `seaborne`.
- **State** — `open`, `done`, or `cancelled`.

## Who uses this section

- **Rangers, Operators, and Coordinators** review patrols and monitor coverage.
- **Coordinators** create and manage the patrol schedule.
- **Site Admins** manage patrol boundaries, restore or delete patrols, and rebuild GPS tracks.

## In this section

- [Patrol Monitoring](/docs/patrols/monitoring) — the patrol list, filters, exports, and GPS tracks.
- [Patrol Detail](/docs/patrols/patrol-detail) — a single patrol's key facts, editable fields, segments, and history.
- [Patrol Area Planning](/docs/patrols/area-planning) — boundaries, municipalities, and protected zones.
- [Patrol Schedule](/docs/patrols/schedule-gantt) — calendar, kanban, map, and Gantt scheduling.
