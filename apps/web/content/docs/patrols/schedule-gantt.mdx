# Patrol Schedule

The **Patrol Schedule** page (`/patrol-schedule`) is where coordinators plan upcoming ranger assignments. The same set of assignments can be viewed four ways, and the schedule warns you when an assignment would double-book a ranger.

## Who uses it

- **Coordinators and above** — create, edit, delete, and re-status assignments.
- **Reviewers** — view the schedule read-only.

## Four views of the same schedule

A view switcher at the top of the page toggles between:

- **Calendar** — assignments laid out by date.
- **Kanban** — assignments as cards grouped by status; drag a card to change its status.
- **Map** — assignments plotted geographically, including any planned track.
- **Gantt** — a timeline of assignments you can drag to reschedule.

A **period toolbar** sets the window you are looking at (for example, a biweekly or monthly span).

## Assignment status

Every assignment has a status: `planned`, `in_progress`, `completed`, or `cancelled`. New assignments start as `planned`. On the **Kanban** view you can drag a card between columns to update its status directly.

## Creating an assignment

The **Add assignment** button opens the assignment dialog. An assignment captures:

- **Ranger** — the assigned ranger (a registered user or a name), plus optional **accompanying rangers**.
- **Patrol area** — the area the patrol will cover (optional).
- **Scheduled start**, and either a **scheduled end** or **planned hours** (the end is derived from planned hours when you provide them).
- **Planned track** — an optional route drawn on the map.
- **Notes**.

## Conflict detection

When you schedule the same ranger over a time window that overlaps an existing assignment, Marine Guardian flags the **conflict** before you save. You can resolve it by adjusting the ranger or the times, or deliberately **override** the conflict — overrides are recorded in the audit log.

## Editing and deleting

Each assignment can be edited or deleted from its row (and from the calendar, kanban, map, or Gantt view). Editing the ranger or the time window re-checks for conflicts; a simple status change on the Kanban board does not.
