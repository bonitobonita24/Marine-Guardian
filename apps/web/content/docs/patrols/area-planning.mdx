# Patrol Area Planning

Patrol areas are the geographic backbone of Marine Guardian. They decide which municipality or protected zone a patrol or event "counts" toward, and they power the filters on the report map and coverage reports. This work is done on the **Boundaries** page (`/patrol-areas`).

## Who uses it

Boundary management is **Site Admin only**. Reviewers can see the boundaries that exist, but creating, editing, importing, and deleting them requires an admin role.

## What lives here

The Boundaries page centers on the **area boundary table** — the list of boundaries currently defined for your tenant. Boundaries come in a few flavors:

- **Municipalities** — the top-level coverage areas (with a land boundary, and optionally municipal-waters geometry added later).
- **Protected zones** — named sub-areas under a municipality, such as an **MPA** (Marine Protected Area) or a **special area**, used to filter events and patrols down to just that zone.
- **Official boundaries** — the generated overlay records that render your municipalities and zones on the maps.

## Key actions

### Create Area

The **Create Area** button (admins only) defines a new area boundary directly in the app.

### Add a boundary from a file

The **Add MPA from File** dialog lets you upload a **KML or KMZ** boundary. Marine Guardian parses the file, validates the geometry on the server, and creates a protected zone under the municipality you choose. After upload it:

1. Validates the name and geometry against the parent municipality.
2. Creates the protected zone.
3. Regenerates the official boundary overlays so the new zone appears on both maps.
4. Backfills historical coverage — existing events and patrols that fall inside the new zone are counted immediately, so you don't start from zero.

A brand-new **municipality** (not a subdivision of an existing one) is created the same way, from an uploaded KML/KMZ that becomes its land boundary. Municipalities are grouped by province (Oriental Mindoro, Occidental Mindoro, Palawan).

### Import official boundaries

The **Import Official Boundaries** button regenerates the official overlay records from your current municipalities and zones. It is idempotent — safe to run repeatedly.

### Rebuild coverage

The **Rebuild** button re-runs area derivation for every event, patrol, and fuel entry in your tenant. Use it after an external boundary import or a bulk change has shifted the map, so every record is re-matched to the correct area.

## How areas connect to patrols and reports

Once boundaries exist, every patrol and event is matched to the municipalities and protected zones it falls within. That matching is what lets coverage reports show **patrol and event counts per municipality** (and per protected zone), and what powers the municipality and MPA filters on the report map.
