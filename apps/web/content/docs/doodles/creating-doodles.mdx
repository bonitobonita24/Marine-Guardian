# Creating doodles

The Doodle tool is built into the map controls on both the **Command Center** map and the **Interactive Report Map**. It is a drawing mode you toggle on and off — the rest of the time the map behaves exactly as it always does.

## Turning the tool on

Find the **Doodle** button (a brush icon) alongside the map's zoom and compass controls, and click it to enter doodle mode. Click it again to leave. While the tool is on, a small floating toolbar appears over the map, and your pointer draws instead of panning.

## Drawing

With the tool on, press and drag on the map to lay down a freehand stroke. Every stroke is captured in real-world coordinates, so it stays fixed to the same place on the map as you zoom in or pan around — the sketch tracks the geography, not the screen.

Two settings shape each stroke, chosen from the toolbar before you draw:

- **Color** — six quick presets (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and white, which reads clearly on the dark basemap), plus a custom color picker for any other shade.
- **Thickness** — three pen sizes (thin, medium, and thick).

Change color or thickness at any time; the new setting applies to the strokes you draw next, and earlier strokes keep the settings they were drawn with.

## Fixing mistakes

- **Undo** removes the most recent stroke, one at a time.
- **Clear** wipes every stroke on the current sketch. Because that cannot be undone, Clear asks you to confirm first.

## Saving

When the sketch is ready, click **Save**, give it a **name** (for example, "Patrol boundary sketch"), and confirm. The doodle is stored with:

- all of its strokes, each with its color and thickness,
- the **surface** you drew on (Command Center or Interactive Report Map), and
- the **current map view** — its center and zoom — so it reopens framed the way you left it.

A saved doodle is shared with your whole site (tenant), so teammates with permission can open it from the Doodles list. Saving does not leave any drawing behind on the live map — the sketch is kept in the Doodles list, where it can be reopened as a read-only preview.
